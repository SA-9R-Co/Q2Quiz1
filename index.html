Architecture



Ancient Greek Architecture

Ancient Greek architecture features iconic temples and columns. The three classical orders Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian define its style. The Parthenon exemplifies balance and proportion.

Ancient Rome Architecture

Ancient Roman architecture is known for its use of arches, vaults, and concrete. Key features include grand structures like the Colosseum and aqueducts. The Romans mastered engineering and large-scale public buildings.

Link

Japanese Traditional Architecture

Japanese architecture features wooden structures elevated slightly off the ground, with tiled or thatched roofs. Shoji (paper doors) and tatami mats are typical elements.

Link

Chinese Traditional Architecture

Chinese traditional architecture features wooden structures with curved, tiled roofs. Buildings are arranged around courtyards, emphasizing symmetry. Intricate carvings and decorative elements are common in temples and palaces.
